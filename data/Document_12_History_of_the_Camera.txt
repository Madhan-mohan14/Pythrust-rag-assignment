
# History of the Camera

## Introduction
The camera changed how humans record memories, explore science, and communicate visually. Photography evolved from long‑exposure images to instant digital capture.

## Early Photography
### First Photograph
In 1826, Joseph Nicéphore Niépce captured the first permanent photograph using a process called heliography. The exposure took several hours.

### Daguerreotype (1839)
Louis Daguerre introduced a practical photography method, producing detailed images on metal plates.

## Evolution of Camera Technology
### Film Cameras
The late 1800s saw the rise of film photography, making cameras more portable and affordable.

### Color Photography
Introduced in the early 20th century, it allowed realistic image capture.

### Digital Cameras
In the 1970s‑1990s, digital sensors replaced film, enabling instant preview and editing.

### Smartphone Cameras
Modern smartphones integrate advanced lenses, AI processing, and computational photography.

## Impact on Society
1. Journalism and documentation  
2. Social media and visual communication  
3. Scientific imaging  
4. Art and creative expression  

## Conclusion
The camera remains a powerful storytelling tool, shaping culture and communication.
